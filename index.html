Welcome

On behalf of the Northwest Filipino American Student Alliance, FASA sa UW Bothell welcomes you to NWFASA Conference 2017! Our theme for the 22nd Annual NWFASA Conference is “Isang Bagsak: Moving Forward in Solidarity.“

“Isang Bagsak” is translated to “One Down” or “One Fall”, an action of a singular clap by a group of people that originated from the 1960’s by the Farm Workers Movement in California. Figuratively, it is a statement representing unity and empowerment.

We are excited and humbled to be part of this experience with you. So whether you are a returning conference attendee or if this is your first time attending the NWFASA Conference, we welcome you!

Sincerely,

2017 NWFASA Conference Committee

Keynote Speaker

Emily P. Lawsin is a Trustee of the Filipino American National Historical Society, co-founder of the Detroit Asian Youth Project, and co-author of Filipino Women in Detroit, 1945-1955. Originally from “SHE-attle”, Washington, for more than 20 years, she taught Oral History, Filipino American, and Asian American Studies in California, Massachusetts, and at the University of Michigan. An oral historian and spoken word performance poet since 1990, she has appeared on radio and stage throughout the United States and Manila. www.emilylawsin.com

Workshops

(Click on a workshop for more info)

Cross The Line UW2 031 Social
“Cross the Line” is a powerful, interactive, and effective activity that builds diversity awareness within a group. We are going to help identify the similarities and differences we have amongst each other. We want to acknowledge and address those differences, but also bring to the surface other differences that we may not have recognized. The goals of this activity include helping participants learn about themselves, providing them with opportunity to reflect upon their self- and cultural identity, allowing the community involved to appreciate its own diversity more and learning to treat each other like the diverse human beings we all are, instead of allowing ourselves to be divided by our diversity. We want to empower the group by breaking down stereotypes and assumptions, produced by our cultures and experiences, and make it easier to know one another as human beings.
Christine Tabila and Kennaro Samrith
TALAGANG SLAYING! UW2 021 Social
As members of NWFASA, we are united through our love and passion for the Filipino culture. An important aspect of our culture is dance; dance is a universal language that brings people of all nationalities together. We will be uniting all of our organizations through this workshop in a fun, high-energy environment. In this workshop, we want you all to "dance" out of their comfort zones, make new friends, and gain new dancing skills. Most importantly, we want you to have fun!
Michael Solitaria, Kyle Dela Cruz, and Chloe Tanega
Pin@y Zumba UW2 131 Social
Need a break from sitting at workshops? Bring your workout clothes, dancing shoes, a friend or two, and let's Zumba! Don't worry - you don't need to know how to dance. You just have to be willing to move and have fun. A few of the songs will be in Tagalog. We will also be learning a routine that was choreographed in the Philippines to highlight people coming together to stand up against violence against women. It has been used internationally and nationally at flash mobs and mobilizations. Feel free to learn this dance and use it for PCNs or other events!
Trisha Fey Elizarde-Miller
Making a Difference: A Top-down Approach UW2 140 Social
Our workshop will focus on providing methodologies and techniques that can help you succeed in goal-setting and goal-achievement. Whether it is reaching that hot summer-bod or landing a summer internship, this workshop will offer tools that can assist you in building a versatile framework and lead you down a pathway to making a difference. In particular, our paradigm focuses on the end goal and how you can make decisions and choices to reach it; AKA A Top Down Approach.
Rachel Ramirez and Kevin Le
F.A: A Workshop On How Our Generational Experiences Influence our Identity UW2 141 Social
“F.A.: A Workshop on How Our Generational Experiences Influence Our Identity” reflects how our varying communities and upbringings allowed us as Filipinos or Filipino-Americans to face our legacies. We will explore how our communities affected our introduction and perception of our pasts and identities.
Alec Cuison, Ian Atienza, Joseph Macalma, Erika Aranca, Alex Yi and Julio Dizon
Dismantling anti-blackness in the Pilipinx community UW1 210 Political
In this workshop, we will talk about ways in which anti-blackness, US imperialism, colonization, and white supremacy impacts the Filipino-American experience. There will be small and large group discussions regarding our experiences with racism and colorism. We will also talk about how we can confront anti-blackness within our communities, and stand in solidarity with Black communities and support their movement and struggle.
Hunter Eider
Through their Eyes to Our's: Creating Unity with an Ocean Between UW1 202 Political
Too often the Philippines seems far away. The people, and their lives seem to be parallel to ours here in the U.S. and we live our lives not knowing the experiences of struggle and joy we may share. However, there is a way to converge our paths to create a unity when there is an ocean between. Come to the workshop to learn about Filipino farmers, workers and indigenous people and learn how you can unite with them to create a change.
Joseph Edward Gonzales and Jhustin Custodio
From the PNW to the Philippines - Typhoon Nina and Calling Youth and Students to Action UW1 220 Political
You are the leaders you've been waiting for. Typhoon Nina was the biggest typhoon of 2016 that hit the Philippines on December 23rd 2016. With over 400,000 families or 2 million effect by the devastating typhoon. The call to support is urgent. Learn about the situation, how you can get involved, and discover that the connective tissue that binds us to our people back home that can spark us to develop into leaders of change we never thought possible.
Kenneth Crebillo
One Billion Rising UW1 221 Political
GABRIELA Portland is a local grassroots organization that is a part of GABRIELA USA. We are made up of Pin@y students, professionals, migrants, and workers. GABRIELA educates the community about Filipina conditions in the U.S., in the Philippines, and around the world. In this workshop, we will learn about what kind of violence Pin@y and migrant women face and understand what the root causes of this violence is. We will also highlight ways in which people have risen all over the world to combat violence against women and talk about how we can take actions to prevent violence against women.
GABRIELA Portland
Take Back Our Education: People First, Not Profit and War UW1 010 Educational
Our parents migrated to the U.S. to seek a better future for us. Many of them sacrifice and work hard to be able to send us to college. But today, higher education is increasingly becoming more expensive and out-of-reach for the current generation of students. Many students are forced to borrow loans, join the military, or work multiple jobs while going to school. This workshop will discuss the inaccessibility of education as well as the importance of fighting for education that is culturally relevant, empowering, and encourages students to contribute to the upliftment of our community.
Nikko Francisco and Henry Luke
Reality of Isang Bagsak: State of the Alliance UW1 020 Educational
We will be discussing ways in which NWFASA as an alliance is really working together towards solidarity and unity. The alliance has been around for more than two decades, and it is important to acknowledge our history, and redefine how we function as an organization. This year's conference focuses on collaboration, and we will be discussing ways in which the alliance has proven or has failed to do this work. This workshop is open for everyone, but it is intended for people who are highly interested in reassessing how the alliance operates and the structure of conference. We are welcoming past, existing, and potential leaders from each NWFASA organization to attend this workshop. We are hoping to have a meaningful and productive conversation about their experiences with NWFASA.
Erick Yanzon and Kristine Maramot
Failure and Growth UW1 030 Educational
Perfection is what we are taught all our lives. Working hard to attain anything was hammered down into our minds from the moment we started our education journey. What happens when we are in a situation where we are not able to succeed? What happens when failure is inevitable? How do we individually get back up and grow from the failure? The perceptions of failure and growth are never a topic that is truly discussed and made comfortable.
Cedric Bella and Stephanie Guray
Kamayan: Taking Liberation Into Our Own Hands UW1 040 Educational
“Kamayan: Taking Liberation Into Our Own Hands” will explore the colonized identity of Filipinx Americans by examining the history of the United States’ colonization of the Philippines. As a result, internalized oppression has been carried within the minds of Filipinx Americans, this is what we call “colonial mentality.” Understanding this concept is imperative when confronting and deconstructing experiences with internalized racial superiority/inferiority. The workshop will utilize collective group learning in order to allow participants to reclaim and retell their histories as Filipinx Americans.
Kriztel de Vera, Jillian Guillermo, Nadine Guyo
Filipino Martial Arts UW1 060 Cultural
In this workshop, we will cover a brief history of martial arts in Southeast Asia/Philippines and how Filipino martial arts has flourished here in the United States. The Filipino martial arts has not been traditionally taught in parts of the Philippines and the adoption of techniques has long been taught in the Armed Forces all over the world. This workshop will also cover basic self-defense and practical applications with some traditional weapons.
Gyles Batara
The Heart of Activism: Taboo or Nah? UW1 041 Cultural
Have you ever been stuck in the hyphen between Filipino - American, or nah? Have you ever experienced racism and micro aggressions, or nah? Have you ever been told to stay away from politics and activism, or nah? This workshop aims to utilize our art and our hearts, from music to theatre, to delve into what inspires us as a community to act, to one day wake up to a realization that collaboration and unity within our community is necessary in order to make a difference in our society.
Nikki De Leon
Don’t Dabog: Engaging in Call-Out Culture and Practicing Humility and Grace UW1 050 Cultural
Have you ever been “called out” by someone and you weren’t quite sure what to do? Or maybe you’ve called someone out and it wasn’t as effectively as you’d hoped it would be. There is a culture of calling people out on college campuses and social media, and it is often responded with defense and stubbornness. However, if we want to make effective social changes in our community we have to engage in conversations with the best intentions and most positive impacts. This workshop is an opportunity to talk about the intricacies of Call-Out Culture, how to respond when you’re being called out, and how to engage with others when you’re calling them out.
Jazz Espiritu, Mark Bello, and Laurie Nilo
